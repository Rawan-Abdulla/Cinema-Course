Cinema in Egypt

"المومياء" is an Egyptian movie produced in 1969 and directed by Shady Abdel Salam, and it deals with the issue of stealing Egyptian antiquities. She won many international awards and was celebrated by international festivals. It is among the top 100 Egyptian films, and it ranked third. It was chosen as the best Arab film in the 2013 referendum. Only in 1975 was the film shown to the public.



"باب الحديد" is an Egyptian drama film produced in 1958, directed by Youssef Chahine, produced by Gabriel Telhami, and starring Youssef Shaheen, Farid Shawky, Hind Rostom, and Hassan Al-Baroudi. It is the first Arab and African film to be presented to qualify for the Oscar. It was ranked fourth among the 100 best films in the history of Egyptian cinema in a poll of critics. The Bab al-Hadid movie is one of the most important Arab films ever. The film was selected in the list of the 1000 most important films in the history of world cinema.



"الأرض" Is an Egyptian drama film produced in 1970 and directed by Youssef Chahine, based on the novel of the Egyptian writer Abd al-Rahman al-Sharqawi, and it is considered one of the most important films in Egyptian cinema. In the celebration of the centenary of Egyptian cinema in 1996, it was ranked second among the 100 best films in the history of Egyptian cinema in a poll of critics.



" كيت كات" An Egyptian drama film produced in 1991. The film is directed and written by Dawood Abdel Sayed, and produced by Hussein El-Qalla, and starring Mahmoud Abdel Aziz, Amina Rizk, Sherif Mounir, Aida Riad, Najah Al-Mougy and Ali Hassanein. The film's story is based on Malik Heron's novel by novelist Ibrahim Aslan. The film ranks twenty-fourth in the list of the 100 best Egyptian films, according to a poll of critics in 1996, and the film won the Golden Award for best film from the Damascus International Film Festival in 1991.



" إسكندرية ليه (فيلم)" It is an Egyptian film by Youssef Chahine, produced in 1978, written by Youssef Chahine and Mohsen Zayed. It is the first film in the series of Youssef Chahine's films in which he deals with his biography, and this film is distinguished by not only telling the biography of Youssef Chahine, but about the way of life in Alexandria during the Second World War as well.



"دعاء الكروان" An Egyptian film that is considered one of the best films made by Egyptian cinema .. It came in sixth place in the list of the ten best films in the history of this cinema, in the poll conducted by the Egyptian magazine "Arts" in 1984. The movie (Doaa Karawan) was shown for the first time in the year 1959. The story, script and dialogue Youssef Jawhar, in association with the director, relying on a literary novel of the same name by Dr. Taha Hussein, directed by Henry Barakat and starring the Arab Lady of the Screen, Faten Hamama.



"بداية ونهاية" An Egyptian drama film produced in 1960, directed by Salah Abu Seif and starring Farid Shawky, Omar Sharif, Amina Rizk, and Sanaa Jamil. The film is taken from Naguib Mahfouz's novel of the same name and is considered the first novel by Naguib Mahfouz to be converted into a film. The film was nominated for the Moscow International Film Festival Award in 1961, and the film was ranked seventh among the 100 best films in the history of Egyptian cinema in a poll of critics.



" زوجة رجل مهم" Is an Egyptian movie shown in 1988, starring Ahmed Zaki and Mervat Amin. Written by Raouf Tawfiq and directed by Muhammad Khan.



" العصفور " Is a 1972 film written and produced by Youssef Chahine. He talks about the stage of defeat after the 1967 war.



" عمارة يعقوبيان " An Egyptian drama movie released in 2006, directed by Marwan Hamed. The film is one of the most controversial Egyptian cinematic works, and its message is nothing but the embodiment of a contemporary reality that boils down to the importance of moving away from indifference so that the modern person in modern Cairo is not like a puppet walking without a mind.



" الحرام " An Egyptian drama film produced in 1965, the story of Youssef Idris, directed by Henry Barakat and starring Faten Hamama, Abdullah Ghaith and Zaki Rostom. It was ranked fifth among the 100 best films in the history of Egyptian cinema in a poll of critics.
