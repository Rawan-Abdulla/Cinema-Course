Cinema in Lebanon

"بيروت الغربية" is a Lebanese movie that begins at the outbreak of the civil war in Beirut, as Beirut is divided into West Beirut for Muslims and East Beirut for Christians.



" سكر بنات" is a Lebanese film produced in 2007, also known as Caramel as an international title. It is Nadine Labaki's first feature film. The film was shown for the first time on May 20, 2007 at the Cannes Film Festival as part of the "Directors Weekly" activity. A viewing of the first experiences.



"وهلأ لوين؟" is a Lebanese film produced in 2011. It is the second feature film by Nadine Labaki. The film was shown for the first time on May 16, 2011 at the Cannes Film Festival as part of the "Look What" activity.



"ربيع" is a 2016 Lebanese drama film directed by Vach Polgorjian. It premiered in the International Critics Week section at the 2016 Cannes Film Festival where it was awarded the Grand Railroad Prize to the public.



"الوادي" is a Lebanese film produced in 2014 and written and directed by Ghassan Salhab. The film was selected for screening at the Toronto International Film Festival in 2014, the film starring Carole Abboud and Fadi Abu Samra.



"حروب صغيرة " Is a Lebanese film produced in 1982, its events take place on the eve of the outbreak of the Lebanese War, where it transmitted the image of the war in its violent and destructive form, and a detailed report on the fall of humanity to the bottom of barbarism.



" أشباح بيروت " Ghosts of Beirut is a drama film that was produced in Lebanon and released in 1998. The film is directed and written by Ghassan Salhab.
