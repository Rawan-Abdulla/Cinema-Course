Cinema in Saudi Arabia

" 1950 الذباب " In 1950, the first Saudi film was produced. This movie stars Hassan Al-Ghanim, who is considered the first Saudi film actor. Saudi Aramco produces this movie, which at the time was largely responsible for making citizens aware of the need for health education to preserve the health of everyone. The work in the production of the movie The Flies took nearly two months, and the duration of the film did not exceed 30 minutes,The idea of the film is to make Saudis aware of the danger of flies to human health.



"1966 تأنيب ضمير" Saudi fictional film, directed by director Saad Al-Fraih, starring the actor Hassan Dardir, and produced in 1966, and it is the first Saudi movie to be shot with a cine camera. It is considered the real start of film production in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.



"1977 اغتيال مدينة " A documentary film, dealing with the civil war in Lebanon, and embodying the ordeal that resulted from the international conflict over Lebanon, and the transformation of beautiful Beirut into a city of ruin, destruction, and bloodshed over many years. This movie was a response to the beauty and loyalty of the city in which he lived for a period of his life. It was shown in 1977 at the second session of the Cairo Film Festival, and it won the "Silver Nefertiti" award for the best short film. It was also shown at the First Gulf Festival for Television Production, it received a certificate of appreciation, and Saudi director Abdullah Al-Muhaisin directed the film



"1980 موعد مع مجهول" The longest Saudi fictional film, produced in 1980, starring Saad Al-Khader, and the film is running time lasted more than two hours, directed by Niazi Mustafa and written by Saad Khader. The story of the movie revolves around a police officer accused of killing his colleague, so he escapes in order to defend his innocence, and to arrest the real criminal. In the end, the hero discovers that the whole story is a representation, as there was no murder case, but rather a knit plan to find out the extent of his police abilities, and his worth for promotion.



"2006 فيلم ظلال الليل " In this film, the Saudi director Abdullah Al-Muhaisin discusses the crisis of the Arab individual and his inability to face the accumulations of his past, the trampling of the present, and his horror of the unknown future that seems to have no hope, dream, or promise to make. The film shows an authoritarian regime and a feeling that the ancient methods that it used for a long time to tighten control are no longer effective, to develop its methods by forming an intellectual cell that establishes an isolated "institute" in the heart of the desert that is made as it is a center for hypnotherapy and rehabilitation through the development of the ability to control In the psyche, and in reality, it exercises anesthesia and brainwashing to destroy the competencies that are outside it.



"2008 فيلم صباح الليل " The film talks about the experience of "Abu Hilal", the famous truck driver and one of the classic characters in the Saudi drama, in a comic framework, with three famous historical facts in the Arab heritage, the war of Dahs, Al-Ghubra, the war of Al-Bassos and the incident of Amr bin Kulthum with Amr bin Hind. Abu Hilal lives with these events and interact with them until they go back to contemporary time. The film enters into competition with five long Gulf films at the Gulf Film Festival, including a second Saudi film, The Forgotten Village. Rashid Al-Shamrani expressed to “Culture Today” his satisfaction with the final shape of the film, hoping that it would find an acceptable resonance among viewers, and at the same time, he thanked the director of the Gulf Festival, Mr. Masood Amar Allah for his interest and keenness to participate in the film in the festival.



"2012 فيلم وجدة " Wajda is a Saudi film directed and written by Haifa Al Mansour, released in 2012. It is the first feature film to be shot entirely in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The film tells a human story, celebrating values such as love of life, perseverance, hard work, and the overthrow of the status of Saudi women, starring Waad Muhammad and Reem Abdullah, who played the role of mother.
