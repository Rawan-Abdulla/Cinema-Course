Cinema in Morocco

"علي زاوا" Prince of the Streets is a 2000 Moroccan crime drama film that tells the story of several homeless boys living in Casablanca. It was awarded in the 2000 Stockholm Film Festival, Montreal World Film Festival and in the 2000 Amiens International Film Festival.



" الرسالة" Is a 1976 Islamic epic drama film directed and produced by Moustapha Akkad, chronicling the life and times of the Islamic prophet Muhammad through the perspective of his uncle Hamza ibn Abdul-Muttalib and adopted son Zayd ibn Harithah. Released in both separately-filmed Arabic and English-language versions, The Message serves as an introduction to early Islamic history. It was an international co-production between Libya, Morocco, Lebanon, Syria, Saudi Arabia, and the United Kingdom.



"حلاق درب الفقراء" A Moroccan film produced in 1982 and it is considered the only film directed by Mohamed El-Rakab. It tells the story of a barber in the famous popular neighborhood, Derb El Sultan, in Casablanca, specifically in the Derb El Bour district, which is considered one of the oldest neighborhoods of Darb Al Sultan. A group of the most prominent Moroccan actors participated in the film, such as Mohamed al-Habashi, Salah al-Din Benmoussa, Abd al-Latif al-Khumuli, Mustafa Salamat and Omar Shanbout.



" فوق الدارالبيضاء الملائكة لا تحلق " A Moroccan film by Mohamed Asali, produced in 2005, with a new directing form on Moroccan cinema, as it was based on an image close to Italian cinema, by virtue of that Italy is the country of residence of the film director.



" الرحلة الكبرى" Is a Moroccan film by director Ismail Ferroukhi, starring Moroccan Mohamed Majd and a young French actor of Algerian origin, Nicolas Casali. The film won the Future Lion Award at the Venice Film Festival, and tells the story of a Moroccan father residing in France who decides to take with him on a trip to the pilgrimage his son, who is growing there and imbued with Western culture, far from the traditions of his Islamic ancestors in the country of origin Morocco.
